# ML20_10 — Magnetic Loop Antenna: 20 m – 10 m

> **Single-loop design | 3/8" copper tube | 10 W max | 5 HF bands**

---

## 1. Design Overview

A single magnetic loop antenna covering the five HF amateur bands from
20 m through 10 m (14.000–29.700 MHz). One loop, one capacitor, one
coupling loop — the simplest possible configuration for multi-band HF
operation.

| Parameter          | Value                                      |
|--------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| Band coverage      | 20 m, 17 m, 15 m, 12 m, 10 m              |
| Frequency range    | 14.000 – 29.700 MHz                        |
| Max power          | 10 W                                       |
| Loop diameter      | **0.80 m** (31.5")                         |
| Radiator material  | 3/8" (9.525 mm OD) soft copper tube Type L |
| Coupling loop      | 3/8" soft copper tube, 160 mm diameter     |
| Tuning capacitor   | Air variable 10–100 pF (single)            |
| Number of loops    | **1**                                      |
| Band switching     | **None** — continuous tuning, no relays     |

**Design rationale:** The loop diameter of 0.80 m is the largest that
keeps circumference/λ ≤ 0.25 at 29.7 MHz (top of 10 m), maximising
radiation resistance across all five bands. At 14 MHz (20 m) the ratio
is 0.117, comfortably above the 0.04 minimum. A single air variable
capacitor (13–56 pF) tunes all five bands with no switching — just turn
the knob.

## 3. Loop Electrical Parameters

### Formulae

| Quantity              | Formula                                  |
|-----------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Inductance            | L = μ₀ R [ln(8R/a) − 2]                 |
| Resonant capacitance  | C = 1 / (4π²f²L)                        |
| Radiation resistance  | Rr = 31 171 (A/λ²)²                     |
| Skin-effect Rs        | Rs = √(πfμ₀/σ)                          |
| Loss resistance       | Rl = Rs × circ / (π × OD)               |
| Capacitor loss        | Rc = X_L / Q_cap                         |
| Efficiency            | η = Rr / (Rr + Rl + Rc)                 |
| Loaded Q              | Q_L = 2πfL / (Rr + Rl + Rc)             |
| Bandwidth (−3 dB)     | BW = f / Q_L                             |
| Cap voltage           | Vc = √(P / R_total) × X_L               |

### Loop Constants

| Parameter    | Value         |
|--------------|---------------|
| Diameter     | 0.80 m        |
| Radius R     | 0.40 m        |
| Circumference| 2.513 m       |
| Area A       | 0.5027 m²     |
| Inductance L | **2.267 μH**  |

```
L = μ₀ × R × [ln(8R/a) − 2]
  = 1.2566e-6 × 0.40 × [ln(8 × 0.40 / 0.0047625) − 2]
  = 5.026e-7 × [ln(671.8) − 2]
  = 5.026e-7 × 4.510
  = 2.267 μH
```

> **Why butterfly?** No wiping contacts means no arcing, no intermittent
> noise, and significantly higher Q. Each section sees half the total
> voltage, effectively doubling the voltage rating. For a single-loop
> design where the capacitor is the only tuning element, investing in a
> quality capacitor pays the greatest dividends.

### Stray Capacitance Note

Typical stray capacitance from wiring, mounting, and connectors: 2–5 pF.
At 10 m (C = 12.7 pF), stray capacitance is a significant fraction of
the total. Account for this during construction:
- Keep leads from loop ends to capacitor as short as possible (< 15 mm)
- Use wide copper strap rather than wire for connections
- If resonance cannot reach 29.7 MHz, reduce stray by trimming lead length

### Tuning Drive

A **6:1 vernier reduction drive** is essential. The narrowest bandwidth
is 15.7 kHz on 20 m — at this scale, bare-shaft tuning is unusable.
The vernier provides the resolution needed for precise frequency setting.

- Bend a 550 mm length of 3/8" tube into a 160 mm diameter circle
- Leave a 10–15 mm gap between tube ends
- Solder one end to SO-239 centre pin, the other to ground lug
- Use 56 % silver solder for all joints
- Position the coupling loop at the exact bottom of the main loop,
  centred and coplanar

### Impedance Matching

The 1:5 diameter ratio provides approximately 50 Ω impedance for a
properly loaded loop. Fine-tune the match by:
1. **Rotating** the coupling loop slightly (±15°) off-axis
2. **Sliding** the coupling loop up or down along the main loop (±30 mm)
3. Target: SWR < 1.5:1 at resonance on all bands

## 10. Construction Guide

### Step 1 — Form the Main Loop

1. Cut 2.55 m (8.4 ft) of 3/8" soft copper tube
2. Fill with fine dry sand, plug both ends with masking tape
3. Mark the centre point (1.275 m) — this will be the bottom of the loop
4. Bend the tube around a circular form (D = 0.80 m):
   - A 5-gallon bucket (D ≈ 0.30 m) can be used as a starting form,
     then gradually relax the bend to 0.80 m
   - Or cut a plywood disc to 0.80 m diameter
5. Leave a **15–20 mm gap** between tube ends at the top
6. Empty sand, blow out residue with compressed air
7. Clean tube ends (20 mm back) with 220-grit emery cloth
8. Tin tube ends with 56 % silver solder

### Step 2 — Form the Coupling Loop

1. Cut 0.55 m (22") of 3/8" tube
2. Bend into a 160 mm diameter circle around a paint can or
   PVC coupling fitting
3. Leave a 10–15 mm gap between ends
4. Clean and tin the ends

### Step 3 — Solder Connections

1. **Coupling loop → SO-239:**
   - One tube end to centre pin (use short copper strap if needed)
   - Other tube end to ground lug / flange
   - All joints: 56 % silver solder, not lead-tin
2. **Main loop → capacitor:**
   - Solder short copper straps (15 mm × 10 mm) to each tube end
   - Bolt straps to capacitor terminals (allows removal for transport)
   - Or solder directly for permanent installation

### Step 4 — Assemble

1. Mount capacitor in project box at loop top
2. Install vernier drive on capacitor shaft
3. Attach main loop to PVC mast at two points (nylon U-bolts)
4. Attach coupling loop at the bottom of the main loop, centred
5. Route coax from SO-239 down the mast to the base

### Step 5 — Test and Calibrate

1. Connect NanoVNA or antenna analyser to the SO-239
2. Set analyser to sweep 14–30 MHz
3. Tune capacitor from max to min — verify five resonance dips
4. At each resonance, note:
   - Frequency → mark on dial
   - SWR → adjust coupling loop for minimum
   - Bandwidth → verify against table in Section 4
5. Target SWR < 1.5:1 on all bands

## 11. Operating Notes

### Quick-Start Tuning

1. Connect coax to radio (RG-58 or RG-8X, any length up to 5 m)
2. Set radio to desired frequency, low power (1–2 W), carrier mode
3. Slowly turn vernier knob while watching SWR meter
4. The SWR dip is **extremely sharp** — approach slowly
5. Fine-tune for minimum SWR
6. Increase to operating power (max 10 W)

### Retuning Frequency

| Band | BW @ Q=2k | BW @ Q=5k | Retune interval |
|------|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| 20 m | 15.7 kHz | 13.6 kHz | Every 10–15 kHz |
| 17 m | 23.0 kHz | 19.6 kHz | Covers most of band |
| 15 m | 31.6 kHz | 26.3 kHz | Every 20–25 kHz |
| 12 m | 46.7 kHz | 38.7 kHz | Covers full band |
| 10 m | 70.2 kHz | 58.2 kHz | Every 50–70 kHz |

- 17 m (100 kHz wide) and 12 m (100 kHz wide): one or two tuning
  positions cover the entire band
- 10 m (1.7 MHz wide): requires 25–30 tuning positions to cover
  the full band; most operators stay in one segment

### Capacitor Voltage Safety

| Band | Vc @ Q=2k | Vc @ Q=5k |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| 20 m | 1 352 V | 1 584 V |
| 17 m | 1 426 V | 1 631 V |
| 15 m | 1 426 V | 1 594 V |
| 12 m | 1 378 V | 1 505 V |
| 10 m | 1 298 V | 1 387 V |

- **Never touch** the capacitor or top of the loop while transmitting
- Peak voltage occurs on 17 m: up to 1 631 V with a Q=5000 cap
- RF burns are painful even at 10 W — maintain at least 0.5 m clearance
- Consider a polycarbonate shield over the capacitor box

### Placement Tips

- Mount vertically, as high as practical (even 1 m above ground helps)
- Keep ≥ 1 m from metal objects (fences, vehicles, metal roofs)
- Null (minimum radiation) is broadside to the loop plane
- Maximum radiation is off the loop edges — orient accordingly
- No ground plane, radials, or counterpoise needed
- Body proximity detunes the loop — use remote tuning if operating
  within 1 m of the antenna

## 12. Heat Dissipation

Power dissipated as heat in the loop and capacitor:

| Band | η (Q=2k) | P_radiated (W) | P_heat (W) |
|------|----------|----------------|------------|
| 20 m | 17.7 % | 1.8 | 8.2 |
| 17 m | 32.1 % | 3.2 | 6.8 |
| 15 m | 44.0 % | 4.4 | 5.6 |
| 12 m | 56.8 % | 5.7 | 4.3 |
| 10 m | 67.7 % | 6.8 | 3.2 |

At 10 W input, worst case is 8.2 W dissipated on 20 m. The 3/8" copper
tube easily sinks this heat — surface area of the loop is ~750 cm², and
copper's thermal conductivity distributes the heat evenly. No thermal
concerns at 10 W on any band.
